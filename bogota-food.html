The Food

Bogotá's cuisine offers a unique culinary experience that reflects the city's diversity and rich history. One of its most iconic dishes is ajiaco santafereño, a deliciously thick soup made with chicken, corn, and potatoes, served with avocado and cream—a true delight for the palate and a symbol of Bogotá's gastronomy.

Another iconic dish is changua, a comforting milk soup with poached egg and green onion, traditionally enjoyed for breakfast in the region, showcasing the creativity and warmth of Bogotá's cuisine.

Additionally, Bogotá is known for its delicious chocolate santafereño, a rich and creamy hot chocolate served with cheese or bread, which is a popular choice for breakfast or as an afternoon snack. These dishes are just a glimpse of Bogotá's culinary richness, offering a unique blend of flavors, ingredients, and culinary techniques that captivate all who have the pleasure of trying them.
