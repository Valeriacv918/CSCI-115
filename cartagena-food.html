The Food

Cartagena's typical cuisine reflects the rich culinary tradition of Colombia's Caribbean region, influenced by African, Indigenous, and Spanish flavors. Some typical dishes include "bollos," which are similar to tamales made with corn dough and filled with meat, egg, cheese, or pork rinds, wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. Another iconic dish is "arepa de huevo," an arepa filled with egg and then fried, popular as a breakfast or snack in Cartagena.

For main courses, the "cazuela de mariscos" is very popular. It's a hearty stew made with a variety of seafood such as shrimp, squid, prawns, and fish, cooked in a coconut sauce and served with white rice. "Arroz con coco" is another standout dish, consisting of rice cooked in coconut milk and mixed with grated coconut, a common side dish in local cuisine.

Among meat dishes, the "posta cartagenera" is highly regarded. It's beef cooked slowly in a sauce of tomato, onion, bell pepper, and spices, served with rice and fried ripe plantain. Finally, "ceviche de camarón" is a fresh and delicious dish made with shrimp marinated in lemon juice with onion, tomato, cilantro, and spicy chili.

In addition to these dishes, Cartagena is known for its traditional sweets, such as "cocadas" (coconut sweets), "bolas de natilla" (milk sweets), and preserves of tropical fruits. The food in Cartagena is a blend of flavors and traditions that reflects the cultural and culinary diversity of Colombia's Caribbean region.
