The Weather

The climate of Cartagena is tropical and warm throughout the year, with temperatures typically ranging from 25°C to 30°C. The city experiences two main seasons: a dry season and a rainy season.

The dry season in Cartagena runs from December to March and is the most popular time to visit the city, offering sunny days and low chances of rain. The rainy season lasts from April to November, with more frequent and intense rainfall, especially in the months of September and October.

Due to its location on the Caribbean coast, Cartagena can experience hurricanes and tropical storms during the rainy season, although they are not very common. The constant sea breeze helps moderate temperatures and makes the climate more tolerable, even during the hottest months.

Overall, the climate of Cartagena is ideal for enjoying the beaches and outdoor activities year-round. However, it is important to be prepared for rain, especially if visiting during the rainy season, and to bring light clothing and sunscreen to protect against the sun and humidity.
