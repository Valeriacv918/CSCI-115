The Weather

Medellín, located in the department of Antioquia in Colombia, has a tropical altitude climate. Due to its proximity to the equator, the city experiences relatively consistent temperatures throughout the year, with an average temperature ranging from 22°C to 28°C.

Medellín is known as the "City of Eternal Spring" due to its pleasant, spring-like climate year-round. The city does not have extreme seasons, and the temperature tends to be warm during the day and cool at night.

The rainy season in Medellín is from April to November, with more frequent and intense precipitation during these months. The dry season is from December to March and July to August, when rain is less common.

Thanks to its mild and pleasant climate, Medellín is a popular tourist destination year-round. Visitors can enjoy a variety of outdoor activities, such as exploring parks, botanical gardens, and natural trails, as well as experiencing the rich culture and hospitality of the region.
